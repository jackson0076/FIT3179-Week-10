In today's world, the internet is a vast network that connects the world together, serving as a foundation for various tasks and infrastructure growths worldwide. Despite the long-existing history, the internet is still a growing field evolving every day. The main focus will be on Australia's internet infrastructure and its performance over the past years, which may help others understand where Australia sits in terms of performance, and where the country differs in comparison to other countries.
In comparison to the rest of the world, Australia sits at an average of 71.87 Mbps download speed, ranking it 76th in the world. The country with the highest average speed is the United Arab Emirates (297.62Mbps), followed by Singapore (297.57).
As of 2024, mobile internet speeds have become increasingly popular as it allows people to connect to the internet from anywhere using their devices. Compared to many other countries, it seems that Australia performs decently in terms of Mobile speeds, but falls behind in fixed broadband speeds (94.33Mbps) . Prominent countries like Singapore and China outperform Australia, suggesting although Australia's mobile internet infrastructure is still strong compared with many countries, but overall lacks in performance.

As of December 2023, the number of connected NBN services heavily outweigh connected non-NBN services, and even some providers such as Optus discontinuing service connections for Non-NBN services. Telstra is by far the most popular with the Australian community due to their high speeds and reliability, and so are preferred by most organisations and households.

The most common speed plan in an Australian household is around 50Mbps, which is enough for an average family to browse freely each month. However, some households may not be able to afford the plans, or might need a faster speed to accomodate additional house members, so 25Mbps and 100Mbps are the next best options.
